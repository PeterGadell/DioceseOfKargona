Our Vision and Mission

The Diocese of Karonga envisions a Family of God filled with the Holy Spirit and living in harmony, peace and solidarity. We seek to provide holistic evangelization through teaching the Word of God and facilitating our development work. Our principle values remain the most important component of our work. These values help distinguish the Diocese of Karonga from the other actors involved in evangelization and development within our jurisdiction. In order to contribute towards the attainment of her vision and mission statement, the Diocese of Karonga upholds the following Gospel values:

Defending the Dignity of the Human Person

The Diocese' Development Desk, headed by Mr Franklin Msiska, works to encourage justice and uphold human dignity by empowering men, women and children to undertake practical development so that they themselves can actively meet their basic needs and mitigate impacts of natural disasters. Click here to read more...

Being on the side of the Poor and Opressed

Lusubilo Orphan Care was established by Sister Beatrice Chipeta, of the Sisters of the Holy Rosary. This community works in collaboration with the Diocesan Bishop to foster an environment where orphans, the needy, and the elderly are fully cared for so that “they might have life and have it more abundantly.”(John 10:10) . Click here to read more…

Promoting Justice and Peace for our Region

The Justice and Peace Desk, Headed by Desk Officer Mr Louis Nkhata, was established with the mandate to promote Justice and Peace and to uphold the dignity of the human person through the promotion of genuine democracy, respect for human rights and freedoms, good governance and the rule of law. Click here to read more…

Accountability in the Use of Church Resources

The Finance section, which is headed by the current DOFIA ,Father Cecilio Silwamba, funds all the activities in the our Diocese. This section has four departments which include The Human Resource Department, Finance/ Accounts Department, Procurement Department, and the Resource Mobilization and Investments Department. Click here to read more…

Exemplary Life by All Agents of Evangelization

The Pastoral Commission oversees the pastoral care ministry, and it does so by coordinating all the pastoral care ministries, programs, activities, and policies of the Diocese of Karonga under the guidance of the local ordinary. The commission carries its mandate to fulfil the mission of Christ, which is to go out and evangelize to all nations (Mk 16:15-16). Click here to read more...

Overall Strategic Goal For the Diocese of Karonga

The overall goal in the coming years is for the Diocese to work towards achieving our goals based on our vision, mission and aspirations while developing a self-reliant Church that is self-propagating, self-ministering, self-supporting and promotes the holistic development of the human person. Bishop Martin's Appeal

Since 2010 the Diocese of Karonga has experienced much growth, but we have also experienced much hardship. This is why we are making an appeal for help in our Diocese. We are asking for help in order to combat the effects of the crushing poverty many of our parishioner’s face on a daily basis. It is with a deep sense of gratitude that we say thank you for the support received. Our needs include:

  • Medical supplies and relief
  • Food and clean water for our hungry
  • Education materials for our youth
  • Resources for our aspiring clergy members
  • Proper shelter and dignity for our most vulnerable

Profile

Monsignor Denis Chitete

Vicar General

Monsignor Denis Chitete is the Vicar General and Administrator of St Joseph the Worker Cathedral in the Diocese of Karonga.

BISHOP MARTIN ANWEL MTUMBUKA

Bishop of the Diocese of Karonga

Right Reverend Martin Anwel Mtumbuka was born on 5th August, 1957 in Majimbula Village, in Rumphi District to Anwel Mtumbuka and Wynes Mbale. He was ordained priest on 31st July, 1988, at St. Peter’s Cathedral, Mzuzu. Pope Benedict XVI appointed Rev. Fr Martin Anwel Mtumbuka Bishop of the new Diocese of Karonga on 21st July, 2010.

Father Joseph Sikwese

Chancellor

Father Joseph Sikwese is the Bishop's and Pastoral Secretary. He is also Parish Priest of St Martin De Porres Parish in Ngerenge.

Common Questions About How to Help

What if I would like to make a large donation?

If you would like to make a large donation, please contact the Diocese of Karonga by emailing us at the email address given in the "contact us" section of this website. We will get back to you as soon as possible. Thank you for your kindness and generosity.

Are there ways to help besides donating money?

Yes, we accept donations in the form of clothes, food, bottled water, books educational items, computers, laptops, medical supplies and children’s play toys. For more information about the needs of the Diocese of Karonga and the logistics of donating these items, please contact the Diocese of Karonga by emailing us at the email address given in the "contact us" section of this website. We will get back to you as soon as possible. Thank you for your kindness and generosity.

Does the Diocese need help in dealing with COVID-19?

Yes, we are in dire need of medical supplies.To find out how to help in this area please contact the Diocese of Karonga by emailing us at the email address given in the "contact us" section of this website.

How can I help grow the Diocese of Karonga.

One of our biggest obstacles to our growth is lack of resources. Through your generous prayers and donations we will be able to have the means to fund our current and future growth. Thank you for your kindness and generosity.

Is there any way I could help the most vulnerable in the Diocese?

Yes, by donating on our Diocese website, you help relieve the suffering of the most vulnerable in the Diocese of Karonga.
